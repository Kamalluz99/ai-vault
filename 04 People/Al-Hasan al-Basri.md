> [!info]
> Al-Hasan al-Basri (21–110 AH / 642–728 CE) was one of the most eminent scholars of the Tabi'un (the generation after the Companions) and a towering figure in early Islamic spirituality and jurisprudence. Born in Medina and raised in the household of [[Umm Salama]] (a wife of the Prophet), he later settled in Basra (Iraq), which became his lifelong base. He met many Companions of the [[Prophet Muhammad]], receiving from them directly, and transmitted an enormous body of hadith and wisdom. He was renowned for his fearless truthfulness, his piercing sermons on death and the hereafter, his deep asceticism ([[zuhd]]), and his concern for the inner dimension of worship. His statement — "Every prayer in which the heart is not attentive is nearer to punishment than reward" — became a cornerstone of the Sufi understanding of [[salah]]. Many early Sufi chains of spiritual lineage ([[silsila]]) trace back to him.
