> Shu'ayb ibn al-Husayn Abu Madyan al-Andalusi al-Tilimsani (520–594 AH / 1126–1198 CE) was one of the most celebrated Sufi masters of the Maghreb (North Africa and al-Andalus), known as *al-Ghawth* (the Succour). Born in Seville, al-Andalus (Spain), he traveled east to study in Fez (Morocco), then further east to perform Hajj and study in the Islamic heartlands. He eventually settled in Béjaïa (Bejaia, Algeria), where he became the preeminent spiritual figure of the western Islamic world. He died in Tlemcen (Algeria) on his way to answer a summons to the Almohad court. His shrine in Tlemcen remains a major pilgrimage site. He is considered one of the spiritual poles (*qutb*) of his era, and his influence extended to virtually all subsequent Moroccan and Maghrebi Sufi traditions. His famous saying — "A seeker is not a seeker until he is able to find in the Quran everything that he desires" — is quoted by [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] in [[The Book of Assistance]] to illustrate the Quran's sufficiency for the heart that has truly tasted it.
