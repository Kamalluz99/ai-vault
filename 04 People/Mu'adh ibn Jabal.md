> [!info]
> Mu'adh ibn Jabal al-Ansari (c. 20 BH–18 AH / c. 603–638 CE) was a Companion of [[Prophet Muhammad]] from the Ansar of Medina, renowned as the foremost scholar of the Companions in matters of *halal* and *haram*. The Prophet said of him: "The most knowledgeable of my community concerning what is permissible and what is forbidden is Mu'adh ibn Jabal." He was sent by the Prophet as a teacher and judge to Yemen. He died young — at around 33 years old — from the plague of Amwas in Syria. He is mentioned in [[The Book of Assistance]] in the context of congregational prayer (Chapter 17): he once led a prayer so prolonged that a man complained to [[Prophet Muhammad]], who rebuked Mu'adh ("Are you a worker of sedition, O Mu'adh?") and instructed him to recite shorter suras when leading the congregation, out of mercy for those with pressing needs.
