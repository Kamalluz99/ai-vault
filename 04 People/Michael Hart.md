> [!info] Michael H. Hart (b. 1932) is an American astrophysicist and author best known for his 1978 book *The 100: A Ranking of the Most Influential Persons in History*. A non-Muslim, he ranked the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ as #1, writing that no other person in history had excelled in both religious and secular spheres to the same degree. He ranked [[Umar ibn Khattab RA]] at approximately #52 in the same list. [[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] cites Hart to illustrate that even secular, non-Muslim assessments recognise the extraordinary influence of the Prophet ﷺ and the early caliphs.
