> [!info] Abdullah ibn Umar (ra) was one of the most prolific hadith narrators among the companions, son of the second Caliph [[Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra)]]. Known for his exceptional piety, scrupulousness in following the Sunnah, and deep love for [[Al-Quds]]. He would travel from Medina to [[Masjid Al-Aqsa]] and reportedly refused even a sip of water during the journey so that his visit would be entirely for Allah's sake alone — ensuring he received the full blessing of [[Sulayman (as)]]'s du'a that whoever comes seeking nothing but two rak'ahs would leave purified of sins. A model of sincere, non-performative attachment to [[Palestine]] and the holy sites.
