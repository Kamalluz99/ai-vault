> Yahya ibn Sharaf al-Nawawi (631–676 AH / 1233–1277 CE) was one of the greatest hadith scholars and Shafi'i jurists in Islamic history. Born in Nawa (near Damascus), he studied in Damascus and devoted his short life — he died at only 44 — entirely to scholarship, producing works of extraordinary depth and volume. His most famous works include *Riyadh al-Salihin* (Gardens of the Righteous), *al-Adhkar* (a comprehensive collection of Prophetic supplications and invocations organized by time and occasion), *Sharh Sahih Muslim* (his commentary on Muslim's hadith collection), *al-Majmu'* (a monumental Shafi'i jurisprudence encyclopedia), and *al-Arba'un al-Nawawiyya* (The Forty Hadith of al-Nawawi — perhaps the most widely memorized hadith collection in the world). [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] specifically recommends *al-Adhkar* as the authoritative reference for the daily and occasion-specific invocations from the Sunna, directing readers to consult it for the morning and evening adhkar and post-prayer litanies.
