> [!info] Dr. Omar Suleiman is an American Muslim scholar, civil rights activist, and founder and president of [[Yaqeen Institute]] for Islamic Research. Born in New Orleans, Louisiana to a family of Syrian descent, he is currently based in [[Dallas, TX]]. He holds a BA in Islamic Studies, an MA in Islamic Finance, and pursued doctoral studies at the International Islamic University Malaysia (IIUM). He is an adjunct professor of Islamic Studies at Southern Methodist University (SMU). Known for his emotionally resonant lectures and accessible scholarship, Dr. Suleiman is one of the most-followed Muslim scholars in the English-speaking world. He is a prominent advocate for Palestine, racial justice, and interfaith dialogue. His major ongoing projects include the [[Al-Aqsa Series]], [[The Firsts]] (companion biography series), and [[The Book of Heart Softeners]] Dhul Hijjah series, all produced through [[Yaqeen Institute]].
