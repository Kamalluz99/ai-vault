> [!info] Muqatil ibn Sulayman al-Balkhi (d. 767 CE / 150 AH) was one of the earliest and most prolific Quran commentators (mufassirun) in Islamic history. He authored *Tafsir Muqatil ibn Sulayman*, one of the oldest surviving complete tafsir works. Though some later scholars critiqued aspects of his methodology, his tafsir is a foundational reference for understanding early Islamic exegetical tradition. [[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] cites his tafsir of [[Surah Al-Asr]] as mapping each verse to one of the four rightly-guided Caliphs — a beautiful framework for understanding the spiritual qualities embodied by [[Abu Bakr al-Siddiq]], [[Umar ibn Khattab RA]], [[Uthman ibn Affan]], and [[Ali ibn Abi Talib]].
