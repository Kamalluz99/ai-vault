> [!info] Asiya bint Muzahim (ra) is one of the four greatest women in Islamic history, alongside [[Maryam (as)]], [[Khadijah (ra)]], and [[Fatimah (ra)]]. She was the wife of [[Pharaoh]] (Fir'awn) — one of the most tyrannical rulers in history — yet she believed in [[Musa (as)]] and was tortured to death by her husband for her faith. Her du'a is preserved in the Quran ([[Surah al-Tahrim]] 66:11): *"My Lord, build for me a home with You in Paradise, and save me from Pharaoh and his deeds, and save me from the wrongdoing people."* The Prophet ﷺ said the angels spread their wings over her during her torture — meaning not that the torture was stopped, but that her soul was sustained and elevated. [[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] uses her as an analogy for the people of [[Al-Sham]] and [[Gaza]] today: the angels' wings over them do not mean physical protection from harm, but sufficiency of the soul and an unimaginably elevated rank.
