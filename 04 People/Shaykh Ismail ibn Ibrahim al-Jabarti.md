> [!info]
> Shaykh Ismail ibn Ibrahim al-Jabarti was a Sufi master cited by [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] in [[The Book of Assistance]] (Chapter 5) for his saying on the night and sainthood: "God has gathered all goodness into the night, and no sainthood was ever determined and formally granted to a saint except by night." This reflects the Sufi emphasis on the night vigil (*qiyam al-layl* / [[tahajjud]]) as the supreme arena of spiritual formation — the hour when the lover is alone with the Beloved, unobserved by creation, in the condition most conducive to unveiling and nearness.
