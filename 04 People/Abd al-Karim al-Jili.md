> [!info]
> Abd al-Karim ibn Ibrahim al-Jili (767–c. 832 AH / c. 1365–1428 CE) was a Sufi metaphysician and theologian from the school of [[Muhyi al-Din Ibn Arabi]], born in Jil (Gilan, in northern Iran). His most celebrated work, *Al-Insan al-Kamil* (The Universal Man/The Perfect Human Being), elaborates a comprehensive metaphysical system centered on the concept of the Perfect Human (*al-insan al-kamil*) as the microcosm of all divine attributes and the locus of God's self-manifestation in creation. His writings are deeply indebted to Ibn Arabi's *Fusus al-Hikam* and extend its themes into more systematic theological territory. Like Ibn Arabi, his technical vocabulary — when read without the guidance of a qualified teacher — is easily misunderstood in ways that suggest [[hulul]] (divine incarnation) or [[ittihad]] (union of creature and Creator). [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] identifies him in [[The Book of Assistance]] (Chapter 7) as a writer whose works should be approached with caution by those not firmly grounded in the [[Shari'a]] and traditional Islamic theology.
