> [!info] Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra) was the second Caliph of Islam (r. 634–644 CE), one of the closest companions of the Prophet ﷺ, and one of the most transformative leaders in Islamic history. Known as al-Farooq (the one who distinguishes truth from falsehood) for his directness and justice. Under his caliphate, the Muslim state expanded dramatically — most significantly including the peaceful conquest of [[Jerusalem]] ([[Al-Quds]]) in 638 CE, which was one of the prophetic signs the Prophet ﷺ named at the [[Battle of Tabuk]] as the second of six signs before the Day of Judgment. Umar personally travelled to Jerusalem to receive the keys of the city from the Byzantine Patriarch Sophronius, maintaining the rights of all inhabitants and their places of worship. Father of [[Abdullah ibn Umar (ra)]].
