> [!info]
> Abu al-Qasim al-Junayd ibn Muhammad al-Baghdadi (c. 215–297 AH / c. 830–910 CE), known as *Sayyid al-Ta'ifa* (Master of the Group), is widely regarded as the most authoritative Sufi master of the classical period and the fountainhead of what became known as the "sober" Sufi school. Born in Baghdad, he was the nephew and student of [[Sari al-Saqati]] and also received from [[al-Harith al-Muhasibi]]. He remained a practicing jurist in the Shafi'i tradition throughout his life, insisting that the spiritual path must be grounded completely in the [[Quran]], the [[Sunna]], and strict adherence to the [[Shari'a]]. His most famous saying — "All roads are blocked to creation except to him who follows in the footsteps of the Messenger" — established the template for orthodox Sufism: no spiritual state, however exalted its claim, has any value if it transgresses the sacred law. He formulated the classical concept of *[[fana]]* (annihilation in God) and *[[baqa']]* (subsistence in God) with meticulous care to avoid the heterodox interpretations that would later provoke controversy.
