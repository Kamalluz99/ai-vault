> [!info] Ibrahim (as) (Abraham) is one of the greatest prophets in Islam, revered as the "father of the prophets" and the patriarch of both Banu Ismail (from whom the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ descended) and Banu Ishaq (from whom the Israelite prophets descended). He is credited with raising the foundations of [[Al-Masjid al-Haram]] in Mecca alongside his son [[Ismail (as)]], and establishing the prophetic legacy in [[Jerusalem]] that would be carried forward by his son [[Ishaq (as)]] and later [[Sulayman (as)]]. The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ leading prayer toward [[Bayt al-Maqdis]] as the first [[Qibla]] affirmed him as the imam of all prophets — the inheritor of both lines of Ibrahim. The Islamic pilgrimage ([[Hajj]]) is deeply linked to Ibrahim's legacy.
