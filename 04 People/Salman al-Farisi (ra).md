> [!info] Salman al-Farisi (ra) was a Persian companion of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, renowned as one of the greatest seekers of truth in Islamic history. Born into a Zoroastrian priestly family in Persia (modern-day Iran), he left home to follow a series of Christian monks across the Middle East in search of the final prophet, eventually reaching [[Medina]] and embracing Islam. His journey from Persia to the Prophet ﷺ took decades. He is credited with suggesting the strategy of digging a trench (khandaq) during the [[Battle of Khandaq]] — a Persian military tactic unknown to the Arabs — which helped repel the Meccan coalition. During the digging, he was present when the Prophet ﷺ struck a stone and three lights emerged, one of which revealed the palaces of Kisra/Persia — the very empire Salman had fled in search of faith. He later served as governor of Al-Mada'in (Ctesiphon) in Persia. Famous quote attributed to him: "Salman is from us, the Ahl al-Bayt."
