> [!info]
> Abu al-Hasan Ali ibn Abd Allah al-Shadhili (593–656 AH / 1196–1258 CE) was one of the most influential Sufi masters of the medieval period and the founder of the [[Shadhili order]] ([[Shadhiliyya]]) — one of the largest and most widespread Sufi brotherhoods in the world today. Born in Morocco, he studied in Tunis and later in the East, where he received his spiritual formation. He eventually settled in Alexandria, Egypt, where he attracted a vast following and died on his way to perform [[Hajj]] at Humaythira in Egypt. The Shadhiliyya is distinguished by its emphasis on remaining active in worldly life while maintaining the highest inner standards — unlike traditions that emphasize withdrawal. His student [[Ibn Ata'illah al-Shadhili]] compiled his sayings and systematized the order's teachings in the famous *Hikam* (Aphorisms). The story told by [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] in [[The Book of Assistance]] — of a student who spent a year renewing his [[wudu]] and was ultimately "made all alchemy" by al-Shadhili — encapsulates the order's teaching that inner spiritual transformation (not external techniques) is the true alchemy.
