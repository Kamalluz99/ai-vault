> [!info]
> Abdullah ibn Abbas (3 BH–68 AH / c. 619–687 CE), known as *Hibr al-Umma* (the Scholar of the Community) and *Tarjuman al-Quran* (Interpreter of the Quran), was the cousin of [[Prophet Muhammad]] and one of the greatest scholars among the Companions. Born a few years before the Hijra, he grew up in the Prophet's household and received special du'a from the Prophet for wisdom and knowledge of the Quran: "O God! Grant him knowledge of the religion and teach him the interpretation." He became the foremost authority on Quranic exegesis (*tafsir*) and hadith transmission in early Islam and is one of the most prolific transmitters of hadith. He is cited in [[The Book of Assistance]] as the transmitter of the foundational hadith on God's recording of good and evil intentions.
