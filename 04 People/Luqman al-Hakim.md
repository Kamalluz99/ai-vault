> [!info] Luqman al-Hakim is a figure mentioned in the [[Quran]] in [[Surah Luqman]] (31), described as a man given wisdom ([[hikma]]) by Allah. He is widely regarded by scholars as a wise sage rather than a prophet, though some classical scholars considered him a prophet. Famous for his counsels to his son preserved in the Quran — including enjoining gratitude to Allah, avoiding shirk, establishing prayer, commanding good and forbidding evil, and being humble in conduct. In the Al-Aqsa Series lecture, [[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] recounts a story of a man called Theon (likely identified with Luqman al-Hakim) visiting [[Al-Quds]]: asked where he came from, he said he came from those who fight their sleep at night calling on Allah in fear and hope; asked where he was going, he said to be with those not distracted by trade from the remembrance of Allah and prayer.
