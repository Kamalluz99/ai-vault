> [!info] Umar ibn Abd al-Aziz (682–720 CE), known as Umar II, was the eighth Umayyad Caliph and is widely considered the most just ruler after the Rightly Guided Caliphs. He is sometimes referred to as the fifth rightly guided caliph by scholars for his reform of the Umayyad state — cutting his own salary, returning lands unjustly taken, ending discriminatory taxes on non-Arab Muslims, and restoring justice across the caliphate. A descendant of [[Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra)]] on his mother's side. Notably, when he came to power, rather than establishing his seat in Damascus (the Umayyad capital), he chose Khunasira — a frontier city closest to the Byzantine border — reflecting the principle that the most blessed part of [[Al-Sham]] is the part closest to the enemy, the frontier of defence.
