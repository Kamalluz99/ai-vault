> [!info] Anas ibn Malik (ra) was a major companion of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, known for being the Prophet's personal servant from the age of ten until the Prophet's death. Born in Medina around 612 CE, he lived to be over 100 years old, making him the last surviving major companion. He narrated thousands of hadiths — one of the most prolific narrators in the Sunnah. He is notable for having prayed toward both the first [[Qibla]] (Jerusalem/[[Bayt al-Maqdis]]) and the second (Mecca/[[Al-Masjid al-Haram]]), which he described as the greatest distinction remaining to him in his final years.
