> [!info] Salahuddin al-Ayyubi (1137–1193 CE), known in the West as Saladin, was the founder of the Ayyubid dynasty and the Sultan of Egypt and Syria. He is most celebrated for recapturing [[Masjid al-Aqsa|Jerusalem]] from the Crusaders in 1187 CE — a victory that united the Muslim world. He was renowned for his chivalry, mercy toward defeated enemies, generosity, and deep piety. [[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] invokes him as a comparison to [[Umar ibn Khattab RA]]: what would be a single chapter in Umar's biography (liberating Jerusalem) was Salahuddin's defining life achievement — illustrating the extraordinary scope of Umar's accomplishments.
