> [!info] Isa ibn Maryam (as) (Jesus, son of Mary) is one of the five greatest prophets of Islam (Ulul Azm) and the penultimate prophet before Muhammad ﷺ. Born miraculously without a father to [[Maryam (as)]] in [[Palestine]], he is described in the Quran as a Word from Allah and a Spirit from Him. He did not die on the cross — Islamic theology holds that Allah raised him alive to the heavens. He is the one who gave the glad tidings of the coming of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ (whom he called Ahmad). The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ described himself as "the glad tidings of my brother Isa." At the end of times, [[Isa (as)]] will descend from the heavens to the white minaret of the [[Umayyad Mosque]] in Damascus, [[Al-Sham]], then proceed toward [[Jerusalem]] to fulfil his eschatological role — defeating the Dajjal (Antichrist) and establishing justice on Earth. He will be buried in [[Medina]] next to the Prophet ﷺ.
