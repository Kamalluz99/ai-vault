> [!info]
> Ahmad ibn Isa ibn Muhammad ibn Ali ibn Ja'far al-Sadiq (d. c. 318 AH / c. 930 CE), known as *al-Muhajir* (the Emigrant), is the founding ancestor of the [[Ba-Alawi]] ([[Alawi sadat]]) branch settled in [[Hadramaut]]. A direct descendant of [[Prophet Muhammad]] through [[Imam al-Husayn]] and [[Ali ibn Abi Talib]], he was living in Basra (Iraq) when the proliferation of innovations, political instability, and theological controversies of early Abbasid Iraq led him to emigrate with his family to the remote and pious environment of [[Hadramaut]]. He settled in the town of al-Hajar and his descendants spread throughout Hadramaut, eventually centering in [[Tarim]]. [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] cites him with reverence in [[The Book of Assistance]] as the trustworthy ancestor "who fled from sedition to protect his religion," through whose *baraka* the Ba-Alawi family remained free from innovations and misguiding passions that affected other factions of the Prophetic household.
