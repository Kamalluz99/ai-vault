> [!info]
> Ali ibn Abi Talib (c. 23 BH–40 AH / c. 599–661 CE) was the cousin and son-in-law of [[Prophet Muhammad]], the husband of Fatima al-Zahra (the Prophet's daughter), and the fourth Caliph of Islam (656–661 CE). He was among the first to embrace Islam and was raised in the Prophet's household. Renowned for his extraordinary courage (earning the title *Asad Allah*, Lion of God), his vast knowledge (the Prophet said "I am the city of knowledge and Ali is its gate"), and his profound piety and asceticism, he is venerated by both Sunni and Shia Muslims, though in different theological frameworks. He was assassinated in Kufa in 40 AH. The [[Alawi sadat]] — including [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] and the [[Ba-Alawi]] family — trace their descent from him through [[Imam al-Husayn]]. His sayings on [[certainty]], reflection, and worship are foundational references in Islamic spirituality and are cited throughout [[The Book of Assistance]].
