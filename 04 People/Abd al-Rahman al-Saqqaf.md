> [!info]
> Shaykh Abd al-Rahman ibn Muhammad al-Saqqaf (d. 819 AH / c. 1416 CE) was one of the great [[Ba-Alawi]] masters of [[Hadramaut]] and a major spiritual ancestor in the [[Alawi sadat]] lineage. He is cited by [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] in [[The Book of Assistance]] as "my master" (*shaykhi*), reflecting the devotional and doctrinal connection that al-Haddad maintained to this earlier figure in his family's spiritual tradition. Al-Saqqaf is attributed the blunt saying on devotional routine ([[awrad]]): "Whoever has no *wird* is a *qird* (monkey)!" — indicating that a servant without structured devotional practice has no real spiritual standing. He is buried in [[Tarim]] and his tomb remains a site of visitation.
