> [!info] Amr ibn al-As (RA) was one of the most gifted military commanders and diplomats in early Islamic history. Before his conversion he was one of the fiercest enemies of the Prophet ﷺ, responsible for leading delegations against the early Muslims. He embraced Islam in 8 AH (630 CE), shortly before the conquest of Mecca. The Prophet ﷺ treated him with such warmth and sincerity in that brief time that Amr believed he was the Prophet's closest friend — a testament to the Prophet's ﷺ extraordinary gift for making every person feel uniquely valued. Amr later led the Muslim conquest of Egypt and served as its first governor.
