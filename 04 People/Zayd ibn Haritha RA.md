> [!info] Zayd ibn Haritha (RA) was one of the earliest and most beloved companions of the Prophet ﷺ. He was formerly enslaved and adopted by the Prophet ﷺ before adoption laws changed in Islam, leading to him being briefly known as "Zayd ibn Muhammad." He is the **only Sahabi mentioned by name in the Quran** (Surah Al-Ahzab 33:37), a distinction that [[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] notes was deliberate — the Quran generally withheld Sahabi names so that Quranic qualities would feel universally accessible rather than tied to specific individuals. Zayd was also the father of [[Usama ibn Zayd RA]], whom the Prophet ﷺ deeply loved.
