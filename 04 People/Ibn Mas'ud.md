> [!info]
> Abdullah ibn Mas'ud (c. 594–652 CE / d. 32 AH) was among the earliest converts to Islam, a close and beloved Companion of [[Prophet Muhammad]], and one of the four scholars the Prophet specifically recommended for learning the Quran: "Learn the Quran from four: Ibn Mas'ud, Salim, Mu'adh, and Ubayy ibn Ka'b." He was the Prophet's personal servant for a time and memorized the Quran directly from him. He became the leading religious authority in Kufa (Iraq) during the caliphate of [[Uthman ibn Affan]]. His transmissions of hadith and his deep knowledge of Quranic exegesis are foundational to the Islamic scholarly tradition. [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] quotes him in [[The Book of Assistance]] regarding the congregational prayer: "We have seen a time when no-one stayed back from the collective prayer except hypocrites whose hypocrisy was well known" — illustrating the seriousness with which the early community regarded missing jama'a.
