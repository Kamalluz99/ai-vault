## daily news — 06-04-26

> [!note]- AI — Trump Signs Executive Order Seeking Voluntary Pre-Release Review of Powerful AI Models
> The Trump administration signed an executive order asking AI companies including OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google to voluntarily submit their most powerful models for government testing up to 30 days before public release. The order relies on voluntary cooperation rather than mandates, and follows Anthropic's April decision to limit release of its Mythos Preview model due to its ability to identify and exploit software vulnerabilities. The move signals growing White House attention to frontier AI risks even as formal regulation remains stalled in Congress. Source: [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/02/trump-executive-order-ai.html)

> [!note]- Tech — Intuit to Cut 17% of Global Workforce as AI Reshapes Enterprise Software
> Intuit announced it will lay off approximately 3,000 workers — 17% of its global workforce — on July 31, and will shutter offices in Reno and Woodland Hills. The cuts are part of a broader 2026 wave that has already eliminated over 134,000 tech jobs, with companies explicitly citing AI automation of content, coding, and customer support roles. Microsoft is separately expected to offer buyouts to 7% of its US workforce by end of June. Source: [InformationWeek](https://www.informationweek.com/it-staffing-careers/2026-tech-company-layoffs)

> [!note]- Macro — Gold Drops Below $4,500 as Strong US Jobs Data Dims Rate-Cut Hopes
> Gold fell below $4,500 per ounce after stronger-than-expected US labor market data reinforced expectations that the Federal Reserve will hold rates elevated for longer. The pullback follows an extraordinary 2025 run — gold's biggest annual gain in 46 years — driven by central bank buying, ETF inflows, and geopolitical uncertainty. Meanwhile oil remains subdued with Brent at ~$61/barrel amid OPEC+ supply restorations and US production growth. Source: [Trading Economics](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/gold)

> [!note]- World — US Tightens Blockade Diplomacy on Iran as Strait of Hormuz Tensions Mount
> The US is intensifying pressure on Iranian ports in a strategy dubbed "blockade diplomacy," while Iran continues to threaten shipping through the Strait of Hormuz. Separately, intensive US-Iran negotiations are ongoing to secure a 20-year pause on uranium enrichment, and rare direct Lebanon-Israel talks are scheduled in Washington. The escalating pressure comes as a planned Trump-Xi meeting in Beijing faces added strain from Middle East energy market disruptions. Source: [World Economic Forum](https://www.weforum.org/stories/2026/05/blockade-diplomacy-and-other-geopolitical-stories-to-know-this-month/)

> [!note]- Malaysia — Malaysia Calls Out Double Standards in International Law at Shangri-La Dialogue
> Malaysia raised concerns over the application of double standards in international law at the Shangri-La Dialogue in Singapore, staking out a pointed position on global legal norms amid ongoing Middle East conflicts. Separately, the US Trade Representative is considering an additional 10% tariff on Malaysian goods over Kuala Lumpur's failure to implement bans on forced-labour imports. The dual pressure — on diplomatic credibility and trade — underscores Malaysia's increasingly complex position between Western powers and Global South solidarity. Source: [Free Malaysia Today](https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/)

> [!note]- Science — Physicists Show Cutting a Single Photon in Two Produces an Infinite Swarm of Particles
> A new theoretical study by Johannes Skaar and colleagues reveals that attempting to split a single photon produces deeply strange results: rather than two half-photons, the act generates an infinite swarm of particles, challenging intuitions about quantum indivisibility. The finding has implications for quantum information and the fundamental limits of photon manipulation. In related physics news, researchers separately demonstrated reversing the direction of energy flow in turbulence, overturning an 80-year-old theory. Source: [Phys.org](https://phys.org/news/2026-06-photon-infinite-swarm-particles.html)
