## A Day in the Life of Muhammad ﷺ — [[Dr. Omar Suleiman]]

*Lecture delivered in [[Singapore]], 15 Sep 2025 · 3.4 hours · [[09-15-25 Mon Dr Omar Suleiman]]*

### Why we study his ﷺ daily life
Every single day of the Prophet's ﷺ life was so intentional that a book could be written about each one. The Sahaba competed to memorise every detail of how he moved, ate, greeted people — because they knew they were watching perfection. We love him ﷺ *for the sake of Allah* — not independently of Allah — and that love demands [[Ittiba al-Nabi|ittiba']]: following him in creed, character, and daily life.

> *"Glad tidings — seven times — to those who believed in me and never saw me."*

### Barakah in time
[[Barakah in Time|Barakah]] with time works exactly like [[Sadaqah]] with money — *"charity does not decrease wealth"* — time given for Allah does not decrease your hours; He expands what remains.

- The Salaf who read the entire Quran in a single day weren't superhuman — they had barakah
- We have more productivity tools than ever yet accomplish less — that is the loss of barakah, not a time shortage
- We time-box worldly goals relentlessly but use *"inshallah"* as infinite deferral for religious ones

> *"We don't time-box our religious goals. When it comes to worldly things we have deadlines. When it comes to religion, we have one word — Inshallah."*

### Practical daily anchors
- **[[Fajr]] in jama'ah** — "breaking the spell"; the single most transformative habit
- **Quran goal with a real deadline** — not "I'll finish sometime this year"
- **[[Hifz and Muraja'ah|Muraja'ah]]** — neglecting your memorisation is like letting a camel loose; it slips away without daily recitation
- **Know your Hijri date** — most Muslims only know the month if it's [[Ramadan]], [[Sha'ban]], or [[Dhul Hijjah]]
- **Day-specific sunnahs** — e.g. Wednesday sunnah dua between [[Dhuhr]] and Asr

### Q&A highlights
- **Love and hate**: let both have limits — the person you hate today may be the one you love tomorrow
- **Depression**: distinguish [[Huzn and Hamm|huzn]] (grief over the past) from hamm (anxiety over the future). Spirituality is *always* part of the mental health equation — but doesn't replace professional help. *"You don't treat hunger with salah; if there's food, eat."* Counselling stigma in Muslim communities is a real problem
- **Balance**: *"Your self has rights upon you, your family has rights upon you"* — balance is itself a sunnah
