> [!tldr]
> Episode 2 of [[Yaqeen Institute]]'s [[Al-Aqsa Series]] shifts from the virtues of [[Al-Quds]] specifically to the virtues of [[Al-Sham]] (Greater Syria/Levant) as a blessed region. [[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] draws from a rich series of hadith and Quranic verses to show that Al-Sham was lit up at the birth of the Prophet ﷺ, appeared to him in visions during the Battle of Khandaq, is designated in the Quran as a land blessed for all worlds, is the heartland of the Believers, the land of the Betrayed where the righteous flee, the land where evil empires meet their end, and the land where the Day of Judgment will begin. He closes with a reminder that attachment to Al-Sham transcends geography — sincerity of heart is the door.

## Al-Sham Lit Up at the Prophet's Birth

The Prophet ﷺ described the beginning of his affair in three phrases: *"I am the answer to the du'a of my father [[Ibrahim (as)]], the glad tidings of my brother [[Isa (as)]], and the light my mother saw that lit up the palaces of [[Al-Sham]]."* At the moment of his birth, a light emerged from [[Mecca]] and illuminated the palaces of [[Al-Sham]] — interpreted by the scholars as a sign that the light of the Prophet ﷺ would take the deepest hold on that region, its grasp on Al-Sham greater than any other land's grasp on prophetic light.

## The Vision of the Pillar of Knowledge

The Prophet ﷺ saw in a dream the pillar of the Book (the Quran) being carried from beneath his head toward Al-Sham. He said — three times — *"Verily faith, when fitna strikes, will remain in Al-Sham."* The scholars interpreted this as meaning Al-Sham would always be a land of the Quran, of its people, and of the scholarship that protects Islamic [[aqeedah]]. [[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] notes that Gaza alone had over 50,000 huffaz (Quran memorizers) before the genocide — a living embodiment of this hadith.

## Three Lights in the Trench

During the [[Battle of Khandaq]], [[Salman al-Farisi (ra)]] was digging the trench and struck an immovable stone. The Prophet ﷺ took the axe and struck it three times, each time producing a flash of light visible to [[Salman al-Farisi (ra)]]:

1. **First strike** — the palaces of [[Kisra]] (Persia) appeared. The Prophet ﷺ said Allah had given him that land. Subhan Allah: the very man in the ditch next to him, Salman, had *fled* Persia in search of faith — and the same palace he left would come to Islam through the Companions' conquests.
2. **Second strike** — the palaces of [[Heraclius]] and the Byzantines, meaning the region of [[Al-Sham]].
3. **Third strike** — the palaces of [[Habasha]] (Ethiopia) and Yemen.

At the moment when the Muslim community faced potential annihilation, the Prophet ﷺ recited: *"Verily the word of your Lord has been fulfilled in truth and in justice."* He was shown — standing in a ditch — every major region of the world that would come under the light of Islam. Notably, [[Abu Sufyan]], who was stalking the perimeter of the trench seeking to destroy the Muslims, would later be among those at whose hands the first openings of Al-Sham took place.

## These Virtues Are Not Tribal — They Belong to the Ummah

[[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] preemptively guards against tribalism. The Prophet ﷺ said: *"There is no virtue of an Arab over a non-Arab, or a white man over a black man, except by taqwa."* The virtues of Al-Sham are not ethnic inheritances — they are acquired by sincere love and attachment. The Prophet ﷺ said *"our Sham"* and *"our Yemen"* though he was neither Shami nor Yemeni: if you love [[Al-Sham]] because Allah loves it, it is yours. There could be a wicked person living right next to [[Masjid Al-Aqsa]] who excludes himself from all its virtues; there could be a person on the other side of the world who will never physically set foot there but whose sincere love earns him its blessings. [[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] notes that more people have embraced Islam after Gaza than at any point in his lifetime — many of whom will one day enter [[Al-Aqsa]] and call it their own.

## What Is Al-Sham? Geography and Etymology

Al-Sham in the broadest historical sense encompasses Syria, [[Palestine]], Lebanon, and Jordan — and by some scholarly definitions extends to parts of northern Arabia (Tabuk), parts of Turkey, and the northern regions of the Hijaz. Three etymological origins are offered:

1. **From Shem (Sam)** — son of [[Nuh (as)]] (Noah); when the Ark settled, his descendants populated this region.
2. **From "shimam"** — a good smell; the land's rich vegetation, figs, olives, and fruits gave it a blessed air.
3. **"Left" or "North"** — the most common scholarly view; oriented from the Ka'bah in Mecca, Al-Sham is to the left/north. The Quraysh made winter trade to Yemen (south/right) and summer trade to Al-Sham (north/left).

Egypt ([[Misr]]) and Palestine have always been intertwined — prophets constantly moved between them: [[Ibrahim (as)]] and [[Sara]], [[Yusuf (as)]] and [[Ya'qub (as)]] — there is a perpetual back-and-forth in prophetic history between these two lands.

## Al-Sham in the Quran: Blessed for All Worlds

Allah specifies [[Masjid Al-Aqsa]] in [[Surah al-Isra]] (17:1) as *"the farthest mosque whose surroundings We have blessed"* — a specification [[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] notes is deliberate: it forecloses any attempt to claim Al-Aqsa's identity is elsewhere or that the Prophet ﷺ's journey had a different destination.

In [[Surah al-Anbiya]] (21:71), Allah says: *"We gave him (Ibrahim) and Lut refuge in the land We had blessed for all the worlds."* The scholars note the phrase "for all the worlds" — Al-Sham is not just blessed in itself, it is a source of blessing that radiates outward. Its prophets, its scholars, its food, its spiritual energy — all serve as a blessing to the entire world.

On which part of Al-Sham is most virtuous: some scholars say [[Al-Quds]]; others say Damascus; others say the frontier closest to the enemy — citing [[Umar ibn Abd al-Aziz]], who chose to establish himself in Khunasira (the city closest to the Byzantine frontier) rather than Damascus, recognizing that the closer you are to the enemy of Allah, the greater the blessing.

## A Land of Miracles — Then and Now

Al-Sham is uniquely a land of miracles:
- **The dead rose to life** there — virtually every prophetic story of resurrection (raising the dead) is situated among the prophets of Banu Isra'il in this land.
- **[[Uzayr (as)]]** slept for 100 years in this land.
- **[[Ashab al-Kahf]]** (the Cave Sleepers) — according to the strongest historical analysis, their cave was in present-day [[Amman]] (Jordan), near Al-Sham.
- **[[Isa (as)]]** will descend from the heavens first to Damascus — at the white minaret of the [[Umayyad Mosque]], the first masjid built during the early openings of Al-Sham — then proceed toward [[Jerusalem]].
- On the Day of Judgment, the Prophet ﷺ will be the first to rise from his grave in Medina, and proceed to Al-Sham for the gathering ([[Hashr]]). Every person who ever lived will go to Al-Sham — either now or later.

To those who ask how the angels' wings can be spread over Al-Sham amid such atrocity: [[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] draws the analogy of [[Asiya (ra)]], whose body was being tortured by [[Pharaoh]] while the angels spread their wings over her — the wings were not protection from physical harm, but sufficiency of the soul. *The light rising from Gaza to the heavens right now is greater than it has ever been.* Do not despair — miracles manifest first in Al-Sham.

## The Heartland of the Believers

A cluster of hadiths define Al-Sham's spiritual position:

- **"Upon you is Al-Sham"** — when asked where to go in times of tribulation: *"Al-Sham, for it is the chosen land of Allah. Allah will drive toward it the best of His servants."*
- **"Verily the heartland of the Believers is Al-Sham."**
- **"Faith will remain in Al-Sham until the Final Hour"** — there will always be a level of religious commitment, scholarship that guards the aqeedah, and warriors that guard the borders. Not time-bound.
- **"The angels of the Merciful have their wings spread over Al-Sham."**
- **"If the people of Al-Sham are corrupted, there is no good left in you"** — Al-Sham is the *barometer* of the Ummah. When the Ummah is weak, you see it in Al-Sham. When it is corrupted, Al-Sham shows it. When it can't act for Al-Sham, it reveals its paralysis.

Evil empires have always met their end in Al-Sham: the Crusaders fell there, the Mongols fell there — and Allah's promise is that all oppressors will meet their end there. The final [[ta'ifa al-mansura]] (Victorious Group) is situated around Jerusalem, and the Prophet ﷺ described them as *"not deterred by those who betray them."*

Al-Sham is not the land of betrayal — it is the land of the *Betrayed*. The prophets fled there after being let down by their own people. It is the land where, standing alone and depending entirely on Allah, a person finds a connection between earth and heaven unlike anywhere else.

The Prophet ﷺ specifically named [[Asqalan]] and [[Gaza]] as the best of ribat (holding down the frontier). *"The best of your holding down the fort is Asqalan and Gaza."* The Prophet ﷺ named Gaza specifically — and it refers historically to a much larger area than the strip it has been reduced to.

## Closing: The Heart Connects Across Distance

A sincere heart can make tawaf around the Ka'bah without the body being there. A sincere du'a reaches the Throne of Allah from anywhere. Likewise, a person can be geographically far from Al-Sham but spiritually bound to it through recognizing its virtues and keeping their focus on it. [[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] closes with du'a: that Allah liberate every part of Al-Sham, be with the oppressed wherever they are, and count among those who attain the virtues of Al-Sham all those who love it — whether or not they have ever physically set foot there.
