### The Book

Full title: *Risalat al-Mu'awanah wal-Muzaharah wal-Mu'azarah Li Raghibina Minal Mu'minin Fi Suluki Tariqil Akhirah* — "Book of Assistance, Support, and Aid for Believing Seekers Travelling the Path of the Hereafter."

The title itself answers four fundamental questions: Who are we? (servants/travellers of Allah), Where did we come from? (from Allah), Where are we headed? (back to Allah, via the akhirah), Why are we here? (to worship and know Allah — *wa ma khalaqtul jinnah wal insa illal ya'budun*).

[[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] addresses the *salik* — the wayfarer. The spiritual journey (*suluk*) passes through stations (*maqamat*): Yaqin → Tawbah → Sabr → Shukr → Zuhd → Khawf → Raja → Tawakkul → Rida → Mahabbah → Ma'rifatullah. The book begins with Yaqin because certainty is the foundation all other stations rest on.

### Three Pillars of Religion (Hadith Jibril)

From the hadith narrated by Sayyidina Umar RA — Jibril appeared as a man to teach the Companions their religion through three questions:

| Pillar | Question | Subject | Function |
|--------|----------|---------|----------|
| Islam | Tell me about Islam | Fiqh | Ensures **validity** (*sihah*) of outward actions |
| Iman | Tell me about Iman | Aqidah / Tauhid | Ensures **soundness** of belief |
| Ihsan | Tell me about Ihsan | Tasawwuf | Ensures **acceptance** (*qabul*) of all deeds |

*"Fiqh rectifies the limbs; Aqidah rectifies the beliefs; Tasawwuf rectifies the heart."*

Ihsan = to worship Allah as though you see Him (*mushahadah*); if you cannot, know that He sees you (*muraqabah*).

Key point: a person can have valid prayers (Islam ✓) and correct belief (Iman ✓) but still not have deeds *accepted* without Ihsan. Evidence: hadith of the woman who never missed prayers but entered Jahannam because of her tongue; hadith of the one who fasted Ramadan but only gained hunger.

**Tasawwuf** (*to purify and beautify*) is not optional — it is *fard 'ayn*, an individual obligation on every Muslim, just as fiqh and aqidah are.

### Spiritual Diseases Mentioned

- **Hasad** — envy/hatred
- **Adawah** — enmity  
- **Takabur** — arrogance, feeling superior
- **Ria'** — doing deeds for validation from others
- **Al-'Ujub** — self-amazement; more subtle than Ria'; Rasulullah ﷺ described it as a black stone in the night — almost impossible to see. Modern name: **Selfism**.

On *self-love*: Ustaz challenged the concept — "Which self are you loving? The ruh (longing for Allah's light) or the nafs (inclining to desire)?" The qalb (*ever-changing*) is the battleground between the two.

**Imam Malik**: *"Man tafaqaha wa lam yatasawwaf faqad tafasaqa"* — One who learns fiqh but neglects tasawwuf will likely transgress. Knowledge without muraqabah leads to fasiq.

### Chapter 1: On Certainty (Yaqin)

#### Counsel (from Book of Assistance)
> "You must, beloved brother, strengthen and improve your certainty. For when certainty prevails in the heart and establishes itself therein, the unseen becomes as if seen and the man aided by providence says: 'Were the cover to be removed, I would not increase in certainty.'"

Without certainty: repentance is weak, worship burdensome, sincerity fades, patience breaks, trust in Allah diminishes.  
*"Certainty is the substance of all spiritual stations and the bearer that carries them."* (Ibn Qayyim, *Madarij al-Salikin*)

#### Definition
**[[Al-Junayd]]**: *"Al-yaqin huwa istiqrar al-ilm al-ladhi la yataqalab wa la yahul wa la yataghayyar fil qalb"* — Certainty is the settling of knowledge in the heart such that it neither reverses, fluctuates, nor changes.

**The core distinction — Ilm vs Yaqin:**  
> Knowledge becomes certainty **when it governs the way you think, feel, and act.**

Example: Everyone *knows* death is inevitable. But Yaqin is when that knowledge of death becomes a factor in your decision-making — including whether to commit a sin right now.

Remark on *rizq*: Rasulullah ﷺ said that if a person were to try to escape their provision, that provision would chase them and enter their mouth. The scholars' analogy: rizq is like your shadow — the more you chase it, the more it runs from you. Walk normally, and it follows.

#### Alchemy of Certainty (3 means)
1. **Reflect upon Revelation** (*Tadabbur & Tafakkur*) — listen with the heart to Qur'an/hadith about Allah's majesty, perfection, truthfulness
2. **Reflect upon Creation** (*Tafakkur*) — it is impossible for this universe to exist without a creator; this reflection rebuts Darwinism and evolutionism
3. **Act upon every knowledge learnt** (*Amal bil 'Ilm*) — become what you learn, not merely know it. *Al-amal ubi alim yurisun yaqin.*

#### Virtues and Fruits
6 fruits of established Yaqin: (1) Tranquillity in Allah's Promise, (2) Confidence in His Provision, (3) Deep Longing for Allah, (4) Freedom from Distractions, (5) Constant Return to Allah, (6) Seeking His Pleasure above all else.

Early scholars: *"Allah has not sent down from Heaven anything more noble than certainty."*  
Sufi saying: *"If kings knew the joy we experience, they would fight us for it with their swords."*

#### Degrees of Certainty
| Group | Degree | Description |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| Ashabul Yamin (Successful Believers) | Faith/Iman | Firm belief, but can be shaken under pressure |
| Al-Muqarrabin (Those Brought Near) | True Certainty/Yaqin | Unseen becomes as seen; doubt no longer possible or imaginable |
| Al-Anbiya' wa al-Siddiqin | Kashf + Mushahadah | Unseen witnessed with spiritual perception |

**[[Imam al-Ghazali]]'s Zayd illustration** (*Ihya' Ulum al-Din*):
- *Told* Zayd is in the room → Faith (Ashabul Yamin)
- *Hear* his voice through the door → True Certainty (al-Muqarrabin)
- *See* Zayd with your own eyes → Kashf/Mushahadah (al-Anbiya')

### Modern Threats to Yaqin

| Ideology | Core problem | Antidote |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Materialism | Only believe what can be seen/measured | Iman bil Ghayb |
| Selfism | Self-centric belief; "manifest your own destiny" | God-centric belief; *walat takil ni ila nafsi* |
| Hedonism | Living for pleasure; desire as king | Taqwa (God-consciousness) |
| Secularism | Separating religion from daily life/identity | Islam as encompassing way of life |

On Secularism: "You can say I'm a Muslim in the mosque. Outside I can be myself." This is the disease — treating Islam as a religion *of buildings* rather than a way of life.

On Depression and Shaitan: Qur'an says *inna mayuridu shaitanu liyahzula ladhina amanu* — Shaitan's main objective is to make believers sad and depressed. Not all sadness is bad, but we must diagnose: is this feeling drawing me closer to Allah (positive) or pulling me toward negligence of worship (Shaitan-exploited)? [[Habib Ali al-Jufri]]: check what comes *after* the feeling.

### Tawakal — Three Levels

1. **Partner in a company** — reliance with collaboration and shared agency
2. **Baby toward mother** — the baby just cries; the mother provides. No need to enumerate needs.
3. **Jenazah with the one who washes it** ([[Imam al-Ghazali]]) — the highest level. A corpse has *zero* desire of its own. Complete surrender: *"Ya Allah, I want only what You want."*

On du'a: Du'a is not primarily asking for things — it is an *expression of servitude*. "Qad kafani ilmu rabbi min su'ali wahtiyari" (Imam al-Haddad: the knowledge of my Lord already suffices; my du'a expresses that I am His servant). The greatest du'a according to Imam Abu Abbas al-Mursi: simply *"Ya Allah Allah"* — negating your own desire entirely.

### Closing Advice

Two keys to strengthening Yaqin:
1. **Never neglect circles of knowledge** (*halaqat*) — social media consumption crowds out the real product Rasulullah ﷺ left us
2. **Seek a teacher with connected isnad** — knowledge is a fountain flowing from Rasulullah ﷺ through a chain of teachers. Self-study without a connected teacher risks contaminating the knowledge. Du'a: *"Ya Allah, show me towards someone who can show me towards You."*
