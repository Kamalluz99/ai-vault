> [!tldr]
> Dr. Omar Suleiman delivered a packed 3.4-hour lecture in Singapore on structuring your day around the Prophet's ﷺ example. The talk weaves tafsir of [[Surah Al-Asr]], biographical parallels from the four rightly-guided Caliphs, a deep exploration of [[Barakah in Time]], and practical daily sunnahs — closing with a candid Q&A on depression, family balance, and managing human emotions.

## Key Topics

### Why Study the Prophet's ﷺ Daily Life
- Every single day of the Prophet's ﷺ life was so intentional that a book could be written about each one
- The [[Sahaba]] competed to observe every detail — how he moved, greeted, ate, walked — because they understood they were watching perfection
- We love the Prophet ﷺ *for the sake of Allah*, not independent of him — a key distinction that prevents elevating him to divinity
- The hadith: *"Glad tidings (7 times) to those who believe in me without having seen me"* — we carry double reward as believers of the unseen
- [[Ittiba al-Nabi|Ittiba']] (following the Prophet) is the condition Allah placed on attaining His love: *"If you love Allah, follow me and Allah will love you"* (Surah Al-Imran 3:31)

### Surah Al-Asr as a Framework
- Tafsir through the lens of the four Khulafa — [[Muqatil ibn Sulayman]] is cited as the source for this approach
- **الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا → [[Abu Bakr al-Siddiq]]**: The deepest iman — if all human iman were weighed against his, his would outweigh it. He never hesitated, said *"Aamantu"* instantly
- **وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ → [[Umar ibn al-Khattab|Umar ibn Khattab]]**: Unmatched productivity. Night patrols in Medina while governing a growing empire. Worried about a donkey in Iraq tripping on a road he hadn't paved. [[Michael Hart]] (non-Muslim) ranks the Prophet ﷺ #1 and Umar ~#52 in history's most influential humans
- **وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالْحَقِّ → [[Uthman ibn Affan]]**: Communal generosity. Bought the [[Bir Rumah]] well for the ummah. Financed the [[Expedition of Tabuk]] single-handedly. Standardised the [[Mus'haf]]. His original endowment still generates charitable revenue in Medina hotels to this day
- **وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالصَّبْرِ → [[Ali ibn Abi Talib]]**: Walked the entire [[Hijrah]] route from [[Mecca]] to [[Medina]] on foot as a death walk, sleeping in the Prophet's bed to deceive assassins

### Barakah in Time
- Parallel to [[Sadaqah]]: *"Charity does not decrease wealth"* — time given for Allah does not decrease your time
- The Prophet ﷺ warned of time shrinking before the Hour — scholars debate whether this is literal (Allah compresses time) or metaphorical (loss of barakah)
- The [[Dajjal]] hadith — his first day will feel like a year, second like a month — the Sahaba's immediate question: *"How do we pray?"* (not about diet or work) — showing where their priorities lay
- We have more productivity tools than ever yet accomplish less — this is the loss of barakah, not a time shortage
- Time-boxing worldly goals vs. using *"inshallah"* as infinite deferral for religious ones — a challenge to set time-bound Islamic goals
- Most Muslims cannot name the current Hijri month unless it is [[Ramadan]], [[Sha'ban]], or [[Dhul Hijjah]] — Dr. Suleiman's challenge: know your calendar

### The Prophet's ﷺ Daily Routine — Practical Anchors
- **[[Fajr]] in jama'ah**: He describes this as "breaking the spell" — the single most transformative daily habit
- **Quran recitation**: The Salaf would complete a full khatm in a day — not through miraculous compression of time but through Allah expanding their hours. Dr. Suleiman mentions his mother completed half the Quran daily in [[Ramadan]]
- **[[Hifz and Muraja'ah|Hifz]] and muraja'ah**: To maintain memorisation you must recite constantly — the Prophet ﷺ described neglecting it as letting a camel loose
- **Day-specific sunnahs**: E.g. Wednesday — sunnah dua between [[Dhuhr]] and [[Asr]]; being aware of what the Prophet ﷺ would have been doing on that exact day of the week is spiritually profound
- **Knowing the date**: Just as you know Monday vs Friday in the dunya, knowing *Rajab 15* vs *Muharram 10* recalibrates your spiritual orientation

### Q&A Highlights
- **Balancing love and hatred**: Do not let either exceed its bounds — *"Let your love have limits and your hatred have limits"* (Prophetic guidance). The person you hate today may be the one you love tomorrow
- **Depression and Islam**: Dr. Suleiman distinguishes [[Huzn and Hamm|huzn]] (grief over the past) from [[Huzn and Hamm|hamm]] (anxiety over the future). Spirituality is always part of the equation of mental health but does not replace professional help — *"You don't treat hunger with salah, but salah helps you bear hunger"*. Counselling stigma in Muslim communities is a real problem; seeking help is not weakness
- **Rights and balance**: The Prophet ﷺ said *"Your self has a right upon you, your family has a right upon you"* — balance is itself a sunnah. Even while feeling grief, anger, or guilt fully, you still fulfil those rights
