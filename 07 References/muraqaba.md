*Muraqaba* (مراقبة) — vigilance, mindful watchfulness — is the practice and spiritual station of maintaining continuous awareness that God sees, knows, and is present with the servant at every moment. The term derives from the Arabic root *r-q-b* (to watch over). In the Sufi tradition, [[muraqaba]] is identified as the station of *ihsan* (excellence), grounded in the hadith: "Excellence is to worship God as if you see Him, for if you do not see Him, He sees you." It is the permanent, lived sense — not merely the theoretical acknowledgment — that nothing is hidden from God. [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] describes it in [[The Book of Assistance]] as "one of the most noble stations, high positions, and lofty degrees," distinguished by the rarity of its genuine realization: every believer *knows* that God observes him; what is rare is that this knowledge becomes a constant felt reality that governs behavior in private as much as in public. The ultimate fruit of sustained [[muraqaba]] is complete absorption in God — [[fana]] (annihilation) — and absence from creation through contemplation of the True King.
