The Umayyad Mosque (المسجد الأموي) in Damascus, Syria, is one of the oldest and largest mosques in the world, built on a site that has served as a place of worship since antiquity (previously a Aramaean temple, then Roman temple of Jupiter, then a Byzantine church). Constructed under Umayyad Caliph al-Walid ibn Abd al-Malik around 706–715 CE, it was the first major mosque built during the early Islamic openings (futuh) of [[Al-Sham]]. In Islamic eschatology it holds a unique place: [[Isa (as)]] will descend from the heavens to its white minaret at the end of times, before proceeding toward [[Jerusalem]]. It also contains the shrine of Yahya (John the Baptist).
