Al-Quds (القدس) is the Arabic name for Jerusalem, derived from the root *qds* meaning purity and sanctity. It is the third holiest city in Islam after [[Mecca]] and [[Medina]], home to [[Masjid Al-Aqsa]] and the Dome of the Rock. In Islamic theology it is called [[Bayt al-Maqdis]] (House of Purity), the land of the prophets, the original land of [[Hijra]], and the land of assembly ([[Hashr]]) on the Day of Judgment. It was the first [[Qibla]] of the Muslims before the direction of prayer was changed to Mecca. The city was peacefully taken by [[Umar ibn al-Khattab (ra)]] in 638 CE and has been under Israeli military occupation since 1967. Its liberation is considered a theological obligation, not merely a political cause, in Islamic scholarship.
