*Rida* (رضا) — contentment with God's decree — is the highest station in the classical Sufi map of the spiritual path, and is treated in [[The Book of Assistance]] (Chapter 32) as the fullness or crown of [[mahabba]] (love of God). It is distinguished from [[sabr]] (fortitude) by a crucial difference: *sabr* is patience despite disliking what God has decreed; *rida* is the cessation of that disliking altogether — the servant accepts God's choice as his own choice, because his will has aligned completely with God's will.

The Quran attests to this station in both directions: God's contentment with the servant ("God is well-pleased with them, and they are well-pleased with Him" — 98:8) and the servant's contentment with God. The gnostics describe *rida* as a profound interior sweetness — to want only what God wants, to have released all personal preference about outcomes, is to live already in a foretaste of the Garden. [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] notes that in *rida* every act of the servant becomes a pure expression of worship (*ibadah*) in the deepest sense, since will and divine decree have converged. *Rida* does not mean passivity or the abandonment of means; it means doing everything in one's competence and then receiving the outcome with complete peace, whatever it may be.
