*Fard kifaya* (فرض كفاية) — collective obligation, sufficiency obligation — is a category of Islamic legal obligation that is binding on the Muslim community as a whole rather than on each individual member. It is discharged when a sufficient number of community members perform it; if no one does, all capable and aware members are held sinful. This contrasts with *fard 'ayn* (individual obligation), which each Muslim must fulfill personally — such as the five daily prayers, fasting Ramadan, and paying one's own zakat.

Examples of *fard kifaya* include: funeral prayers (*salat al-janaza*), the religious education of the community, the preservation of the Islamic sciences, medical and judicial services, and — critically for [[The Book of Assistance]] — [[amr bil-maruf wa nahy an al-munkar]] (enjoining good and forbidding evil). [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] explains in Chapter 22 that this duty is *fard kifaya*: if some members of the community discharge it, the rest are exempt, and its reward belongs specifically to those who fulfilled it; but if no one acts, everyone who is aware and capable shares in the sin of omission.
