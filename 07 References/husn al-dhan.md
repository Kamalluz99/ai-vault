*Husn al-dhan* (حسن الظن) — good opinion, thinking well of others — is the Islamic ethic of interpreting a fellow Muslim's ambiguous acts in the most favorable possible light, and of maintaining a positive assumption about their character and intentions absent clear evidence of wrongdoing. The Quran commands: "O you who believe! Avoid much suspicion; indeed, some suspicion is sin" (49:12). In *hadith*: "Beware of suspicion, for suspicion is the most false of speech." The opposite — *su' al-dhan* (ill opinion) — is condemned as a form of injustice when directed at Muslims without evidence.

[[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] covers *husn al-dhan* in Chapter 26 of [[The Book of Assistance]] alongside the spread of the [[salam]] greeting — both are manifestations of the broader duty of love toward one's Muslim brothers. He pairs it with the prohibition of spying: "The one who seeks out the secrets of his brother Muslim will have God seeking out his secrets until He exposes him." *Husn al-dhan* does not require ignoring clear evidence of wrongdoing, but it demands that where ambiguity exists, the believer defaults to the charitable interpretation. This is especially important given the harm that baseless suspicion causes to brotherhood and community cohesion.
