**The Hijrah** (هجرة, "migration") refers to the Prophet Muhammad's ﷺ migration from [[Mecca]] to [[Medina]] in 622 CE. This event marks the beginning of the Islamic (Hijri) calendar and represents a pivotal turning point — the establishment of the first Muslim community-state.

**Ali ibn Abi Talib's Hijrah** (mentioned in [[09-15-25 Mon Dr Omar Suleiman]]):
The Prophet ﷺ asked [[Ali ibn Abi Talib]] to sleep in his bed the night the Quraysh planned to assassinate him, wearing the Prophet's green cloak as a decoy. Ali agreed — essentially volunteering for potential death. He then completed the entire Hijrah route on foot, arriving in Medina after an exhausting and dangerous journey. This act exemplifies the *tawassaw bis-sabr* (patient sacrifice for the community) associated with Ali in [[Surah Al-Asr]].
