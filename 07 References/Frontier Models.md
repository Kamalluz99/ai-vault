Frontier models are the most capable [[LLM]]s available at any point in time — typically the largest, most expensive, and most capable models from leading labs. Examples include [[Claude]] Opus (Anthropic), GPT-4o (OpenAI), and Gemini Ultra (Google DeepMind). They sit at the frontier of what AI can do, hence the name. In agentic security contexts, frontier models are preferred over weaker models because their superior instruction-following and reasoning make them harder to manipulate via [[prompt injection]] attacks. Trade-off: higher cost per token compared to smaller or [[open source models]].
