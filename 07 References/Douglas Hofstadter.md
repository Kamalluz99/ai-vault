Douglas Hofstadter (born 1945) is an American cognitive scientist, philosopher, and author best known for *[[Gödel, Escher, Bach]]: An Eternal Golden Braid* (1979), which won the Pulitzer Prize for General Nonfiction. His work centres on the nature of consciousness, self-reference, analogy, and the emergence of mind from physical processes. He is a professor of cognitive and computer science at Indiana University. His follow-up book *[[I Am a Strange Loop]]* (2007) distils the core thesis of GEB into a more personal argument that the self is a self-referential pattern — a strange loop — arising in the brain. His ideas have been influential in AI, philosophy of mind, and mathematical logic.
