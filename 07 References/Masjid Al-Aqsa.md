Masjid Al-Aqsa (المسجد الأقصى — "the farthest mosque") is one of the three masjids toward which travel is especially virtuous in Islam, alongside [[Al-Masjid al-Haram]] (Mecca) and [[Masjid al-Nabawi]] (Medina). Located in [[Al-Quds]] (Jerusalem), it is the second masjid built on Earth — 40 years after [[Al-Masjid al-Haram]]. It was first built by [[Sheth (as)]], raised by [[Ibrahim (as)]], and completed in its major construction by [[Sulayman (as)]]. Prayer within its precincts is reported to carry the reward of 500 prayers. [[Sulayman (as)]] made du'a upon its completion that all who come seeking only two rak'ahs for Allah's sake would leave purified of sin. The Prophet ﷺ was taken here during the [[Isra wal-Miraj]] and led all the prophets in prayer. It has been under Israeli occupation since 1967.
