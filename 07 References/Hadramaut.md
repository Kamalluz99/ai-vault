Hadramaut is a historical region in the southerly part of the Arabian Peninsula, corresponding roughly to the eastern part of modern-day Yemen. Its inland valleys — particularly the Wadi Hadramaut — have been continuously inhabited for millennia and produced a distinctive civilization noted for trade, seafaring, and Islamic scholarship. From the tenth century CE onward, Hadramaut became the heartland of the [[Alawi sadat]] ([[Ba-Alawi]]) — the descendants of [[Prophet Muhammad]] through [[Imam al-Husayn]] who migrated from Iraq and established a tradition of Islamic scholarship and spirituality that profoundly influenced Islam in East Africa, South and Southeast Asia. The town of [[Tarim]] — within Wadi Hadramaut — is historically considered one of the greatest centers of Islamic scholarship in the Arabian Peninsula, known for the density of its mosques, scholars, and [[gnostics]]. [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] was born, lived, and died in [[Tarim]], Hadramaut.
