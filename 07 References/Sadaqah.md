**Sadaqah** (صدقة) refers to voluntary charitable giving in Islam — distinct from the obligatory [[Zakat]]. It can be financial or non-financial (a smile, removing harm from a path, a kind word are all sadaqah).

**Core principle**: *"Charity does not decrease wealth."* (Hadith — Sahih Muslim) — what you give for Allah's sake, He returns multiplied. This is the foundational analogy [[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] uses for [[Barakah in Time]]: just as sadaqah doesn't shrink your wealth, time spent for Allah doesn't shrink your hours.

**Sadaqah Jariyah** (ongoing charity) — a form that continues to benefit the giver after death: endowments, mosques, knowledge that is acted upon. [[Uthman ibn Affan]]'s well purchase in [[Medina]] is a famous example — still generating charitable revenue 1,400+ years later.
