Al-Sham (الشام) is the Arabic name for the Levant or Greater Syria — historically encompassing present-day Syria, [[Palestine]], Lebanon, and Jordan, with some scholarly definitions extending to parts of northern Arabia (Tabuk) and southern Turkey. Its three proposed etymologies: (1) from Shem (Sam), son of [[Nuh (as)]], whose descendants populated the region; (2) from "shimam" (good smell) of its rich vegetation; (3) most commonly, "left" or "north" — oriented from the [[Ka'bah]] in Mecca, Al-Sham is to the left/north, while Yemen is to the right/south. In hadith, Al-Sham is described as the chosen land of Allah, the heartland of the Believers, a land of miracles, the land of [[Hashr]] (final gathering), and the land where faith will always remain until the Final Hour.
