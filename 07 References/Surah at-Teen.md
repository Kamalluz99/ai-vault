Surah at-Teen (سورة التين — "The Fig") is the 95th chapter of the [[Quran]], a short Meccan surah of 8 verses. Allah swears by four things: at-Teen (the fig), az-Zaytun (the olive), Mount Sinai (Tur Sineen), and "this city of security" (Mecca). Many classical scholars of tafsir interpret at-Teen and az-Zaytun as referring to the blessed land of [[Palestine]] — specifically the Mountain of the Fig and the land rich in olive trees, associated with the prophets of Banu Isra'il and with [[Jerusalem]]. The surah then swears by these signs to affirm that Allah created the human being in the best of forms, and that those who believe and do good deeds will have an unceasing reward.
