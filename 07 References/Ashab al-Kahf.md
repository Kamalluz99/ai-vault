Ashab al-Kahf (أصحاب الكهف — People of the Cave) are a group of young believers mentioned in [[Surah al-Kahf]] (18:9–26) who fled persecution by hiding in a cave, where Allah caused them to sleep for 309 years before they awoke. Their story is a parable of steadfast faith in the face of oppression and divine care for the righteous. The Quran is deliberately vague about their exact number (some said three, some five, some seven — Allah says the number is known only to a few). Their cave is widely identified by Islamic scholars with a site near present-day [[Amman]], Jordan — in the broader region of [[Al-Sham]] — making them one of the miracles associated with that blessed land.
