**Hifz** (حفظ) is the memorisation of the Quran. A person who has memorised the full Quran is a **hafiz** (m) or **hafiza** (f).

**Muraja'ah** (مراجعة) is the ongoing revision and review required to maintain hifz. The Prophet ﷺ compared neglecting Quran memorisation to letting a camel loose — it slips away if not actively maintained through constant recitation.

## The spiritual discipline
- Muraja'ah requires daily recitation; it is not a one-time achievement
- [[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] notes that the Salaf who completed a full khatm (recitation of the entire Quran) in a single day did so through [[Barakah in Time]] — not superhuman effort, but divine expansion of their hours
- His own mother completed half the Quran daily during [[Ramadan]]

## Practical guidance from the lecture
- Treat Quran goals like worldly deadlines: *"I will complete X juz by end of this month"*
- Awareness of your place in the Quran (how far through you are at a given date) builds urgency
- [[Fajr]] prayer anchors the day; Quran recitation flows most naturally from that early anchor
