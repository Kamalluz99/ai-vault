**Al-Masih al-Dajjal** (المسيح الدجال, "the Deceiving Messiah") is one of the major signs before the Day of Judgment in Islamic eschatology. He is a figure who will emerge and claim to be divine, possessing extraordinary abilities that deceive most of humanity.

**Relevant hadith on time** (cited in [[09-15-25 Mon Dr Omar Suleiman]]):
> *"His first day will be like a year, his second day like a month, his third day like a week, and his remaining days like your normal days."* (Sahih Muslim)

When the Sahaba asked the Prophet ﷺ how to pray during that first "year-long" day, he instructed them to calculate — demonstrating that even then, the five daily prayers remain obligatory but must be estimated. Scholars use this hadith as evidence that time itself can literally change, not merely feel different — supporting the view that the loss of barakah in time near the Hour involves actual, not just perceived, compression.
