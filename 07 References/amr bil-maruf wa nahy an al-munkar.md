*Amr bil-ma'ruf wa nahy an al-munkar* (الأمر بالمعروف والنهي عن المنكر) — enjoining good and forbidding evil — is one of the defining obligations of the Muslim community and a recurring Quranic theme. The Quran attributes this quality to the believers before even mentioning their prayer or zakat (9:71), and curses those who abandoned it among previous communities (5:78–79). The Prophet's hadith establishes the three-level framework: change evil "with your hand" (by direct action), "with your tongue" (by speech), and "with your heart" (by inward disapproval) — "which is the weakest degree of faith."

[[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] calls this duty "the pivot around which religion revolves, and the reason God revealed His Books and sent His Messengers" in Chapter 22 of [[The Book of Assistance]]. It is a [[fard kifaya]] (collective obligation). The practical escalation: first inform and make the person aware, then counsel and put fear into them, then — if able and authorized — coerce them. Silence is permitted when words will not be heeded and harm will result, but silence from self-interest ([[dissimulation]]) is a crime. The duty must be discharged sincerely for God, gently, and with compassion. See also: [[nasiha]], [[fard kifaya]], [[taqwa]].
