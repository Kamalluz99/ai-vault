*Ihsan* (إحسان) — excellence, beautiful conduct, doing good — operates on two inseparable levels in Islamic teaching. The first is the **spiritual station of excellence in worship**, defined in the foundational hadith of Jibril: "Excellence is to worship God as if you see Him, for if you do not see Him, He sees you." This is the station of [[muraqaba]] — the permanent, felt awareness of God's observation — and is considered the highest level of the religion (above *islam*, outward submission, and *iman*, inward belief). The second is **active goodness and generosity toward others** — the dimension covered by [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] in Chapter 24 of [[The Book of Assistance]] (On Kindness and Charity), where *ihsan* means relieving others' hardship, concealing their faults, and actively working for their welfare.

The Quran commands *ihsan* in both senses: "God commands justice, *ihsan*, and giving to relatives" (16:90). The three pillars of Islam — *islam*, *iman*, *ihsan* — form the complete structure of religion as outlined in the Hadith of Jibril, with *ihsan* as the inner perfection that crowns the other two.
