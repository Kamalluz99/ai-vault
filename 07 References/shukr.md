*Shukr* (شكر) — gratitude — is the response of the servant to God's infinite and unceasing blessings, and one of the pivotal stations on the spiritual path. The Quran issues both the command and the reciprocal promise: "Give thanks to Me and to your parents" (31:14), and "If you are grateful I shall increase you; but if you are ungrateful, My chastisement is severe" (14:7). The Quranic challenge — "Should you number the favours of God, you would not be able to do so" (14:34) — establishes that gratitude can never be fully discharged, but the attempt itself constitutes worship.

Classical scholars identify three dimensions of *shukr*: with the **heart** — acknowledging inwardly that every blessing comes exclusively from God, not from one's own ability or merit; with the **tongue** — verbal praise (*hamd*, *tahmid*), saying *alhamdu lillah*, and speaking of God's blessings rather than concealing them; with the **limbs** — using every blessing in God's obedience rather than disobedience. *Shukr* for the intellect means using it to know God; *shukr* for health means using it in worship; *shukr* for wealth means spending it in God's cause. [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] dedicates Chapter 29 of [[The Book of Assistance]] to *shukr*, and pairs it with [[sabr]] as the twin pillars of the believer's response to all circumstances — gratitude in ease, patience in hardship.
