The Prophet ﷺ distinguished two primary forms of sadness:

| Term | Arabic | Meaning |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Huzn** | حزن | Grief over what has already passed — mourning a loss |
| **Hamm** | همّ | Anxiety over what is yet to come — paralysing worry about the future |

A famous Prophetic dua seeks protection from both: *"Allahumma inni a'udhu bika min al-hamm wa al-huzn..."*

## Islam and depression — Dr. Omar Suleiman's framework
([[09-15-25 Mon Dr Omar Suleiman]])

1. **Spirituality is always part of the equation of mental health** — faith, prayer, dua, and community are genuine therapeutic forces, not just comfort
2. **Spirituality does not replace other means** — *"You don't treat hunger with salah; if there's food, eat."* If professional help is needed, seeking it is itself following the principle that your self has rights upon you
3. **Counselling stigma is a real problem** in Muslim communities — seeking help is not weakness
4. **The Prophet ﷺ himself experienced profound sadness** (the Year of Grief, *'Aam al-Huzn*) — his example shows that fully feeling human emotion is not contrary to tawakkul (trust in Allah)
5. **Rights still apply during difficulty** — even while grieving, your family's rights and your own rights must be balanced; balance is itself a sunnah
