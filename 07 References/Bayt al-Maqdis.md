Bayt al-Maqdis (بيت المقدس — "House of Purity/Sanctity") is the classical Arabic name for Jerusalem and its holy sanctuary, particularly [[Masjid Al-Aqsa]]. The name shares the root *qds* with [[Al-Quds]]. It is the first [[Qibla]] of Islam and is described in hadith as the land of [[Hijra]], the land of assembly on the Day of Judgment, and a place purified from [[shirk]] — where polytheism will ultimately not be permitted to settle permanently. The Quran references it in [[Surah al-Isra]] (17:1) as the destination of the Prophet ﷺ during the [[Isra wal-Miraj]]: *"to the farthest mosque whose surroundings We have blessed."*
