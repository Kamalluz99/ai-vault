*Niyyah* (نية) — intention — is the inward orientation and purpose behind an act, without which no deed has religious value. The foundational hadith is axiomatic in Islamic law and ethics: "Deeds are only according to intentions (*niyyat*); each man has that which he intended." [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] devotes Chapter 2 of [[The Book of Assistance]] entirely to *niyyah*, calling it "the basis of deeds" — the same act is good or ugly, rewarded or unrewarded, according to whether the intention behind it is sincere or corrupt.

A sincere *niyyah* can transform a merely licit act into a devotional one — eating with the intention of gaining strength for worship becomes worship; sleeping with one's spouse intending a righteous child becomes worship. Conversely, a malicious intention spoils a good deed entirely. Multiple good intentions stacked on a single act multiply its reward: reading the [[Quran]] with simultaneous intentions of communing with God, extracting knowledge, and benefiting listeners earns the full reward for each. In Islamic jurisprudence (*fiqh*), a specific verbal or mental *niyyah* before each act of worship is required for its validity. See also: [[yaqin]], [[ihsan]], [[muraqaba]].
