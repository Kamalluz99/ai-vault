*Nasiha* (نصيحة) — sincere counsel, genuine good-wishing — is encapsulated in one of the most famous hadiths: "Religion is nasiha." When asked "For whom?", the Prophet replied: "For God, His Book, His Messenger, the leaders of the Muslims, and ordinary Muslims." The Arabic root *n-s-h* originally means "to purify" or "to stitch/repair" — *nasiha* is both the act of sincerely counseling another for their genuine good, and the quality of sincerity that motivates the counsel. It is distinguished from criticism or humiliation by its orientation: *nasiha* seeks the benefit of the one counseled, not the satisfaction of the one giving counsel.

[[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] covers *nasiha* in Chapter 25 of [[The Book of Assistance]], specifying its conditions: sincerity, gentleness, privacy (when possible), and genuine orientation toward the other's benefit. Effective *nasiha* requires the right manner — not harshness or public shaming. The entire Book of Assistance is itself an act of *nasiha* — al-Haddad frames it as counsel simultaneously addressed to himself, his requesting brother, and every reader.
