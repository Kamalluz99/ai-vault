*Kashf* (كشف) — unveiling, spiritual disclosure — is the Sufi term for the direct perception of realities that are ordinarily hidden from ordinary sensory or rational cognition. The word literally means "lifting the veil." It refers to experiential knowledge that comes to the heart not through argument or study but through purification, sustained [[dhikr]], and God's direct gift — a form of knowing that is certain and immediate, not inferential. Its companion concept [[mushahada]] (contemplation/witnessing) denotes the active state of perceiving these realities directly. Together, *kashf* and *mushahada* constitute *haqq al-yaqin* (the third and highest degree of [[yaqin]]) — the certainty of the Prophets and the [[siddiqun]], in which the unseen becomes fully seen.

[[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] references *kashf* and *mushahada* in [[The Book of Assistance]] (Chapter 1) when describing the third degree of certainty, and in Chapter 3 when describing the ultimate fruit of [[muraqaba]]. He also notes that God's friends ([[awliya]]) "attained to the secrets of God's Mulk and Malakut" (the visible and invisible kingdoms) "through unveiling and contemplation." *Kashf* is not a criterion of spiritual superiority on its own — it must be accompanied by and never contradict strict adherence to the [[Quran]] and [[Sunna]]. See also: [[fana]], [[muraqaba]], [[yaqin]].
