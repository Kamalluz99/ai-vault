*Yaqin* (يقين) — certainty — is one of the most foundational concepts in Islamic spiritual psychology. It refers to a degree of conviction so firm and unshakeable that doubt becomes impossible; not merely intellectual assent but a state of heart in which faith is fully settled and its opposite is inconceivable. [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] opens [[The Book of Assistance]] with [[yaqin]] as the root from which all other virtues branch, quoting [[Luqman al-Hakim]]: "Action is possible only in the presence of certainty," and the hadith: "Certainty is the whole of faith."

Classical scholars distinguish three degrees of *yaqin*: *'ilm al-yaqin* (certainty through knowledge/argument — like knowing fire exists from its smoke), *'ayn al-yaqin* (certainty through direct witnessing — like seeing the fire itself), and *haqq al-yaqin* (certainty through direct experience — like being inside the fire). These correspond to different spiritual stations: the first to ordinary believers, the second to the gnostic saints, and the third to the Prophets. The stronger one's [[yaqin]], the more consistent and complete one's worship, and the less power [[Shaytan]] has over one.
