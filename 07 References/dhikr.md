*Dhikr* (ذكر) — remembrance of God — is the practice of invoking God through specific formulas, Quranic verses, or simply by directing the heart and tongue toward the consciousness of His presence. The Quran commands it repeatedly and emphatically: "Remember Me and I shall remember you" (2:152); "O you who believe! Remember God abundantly!" (33:41); "Remember God while standing, sitting, and on your sides" (3:103). In the Sufi tradition, [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] describes [[dhikr]] in [[The Book of Assistance]] as "the pillar of the Path, the key to realization, the weapon of the seeker, and the unfolding of sainthood."

Common *dhikr* formulas include: *tasbih* (Subhan Allah — Transcendent is God), *tahmid* (Alhamdu lillah — Praise belongs to God), *tahlil* (La ilaha illallah — there is no god but God), *takbir* (Allahu Akbar — God is Greatest), and *salawat* (invoking blessings upon the Prophet). The best *dhikr* involves both tongue and heart; the heart should be aware of the meaning of what the tongue says. Perseverance in *dhikr* with sincerity and courtesy yields progressive spiritual fruits: the polishing of the heart, the descent of the lights of nearness (*qurb*), and ultimately the unveiling of the unseen.
