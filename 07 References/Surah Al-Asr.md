Surah Al-Asr (Chapter 103) is one of the shortest chapters of the Quran — three verses — yet Imam al-Shafi'i said: *"If Allah had only revealed Surah Al-Asr, it would have been sufficient as a guide for humanity."*

**The four components of the one who is not at loss:**
1. **آمَنُوا** — They believe (iman, deep faith)
2. **وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ** — They do righteous deeds (consistent, motivated action)
3. **وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالْحَقِّ** — They enjoin truth upon one another (communal propagation of good)
4. **وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالصَّبْرِ** — They enjoin patience upon one another (communal accountability and encouragement)

**Tafsir through the four Khulafa al-Rashidun** (via [[Muqatil ibn Sulayman]]):
- Verse 3a (iman) → [[Abu Bakr al-Siddiq]] — greatest faith of any non-prophet
- Verse 3b (amal al-salih) → [[Umar ibn Khattab RA]] — unmatched righteous productivity
- Verse 3c (tawassaw bil-haqq) → [[Uthman ibn Affan]] — communal generosity and establishing good
- Verse 3d (tawassaw bis-sabr) → [[Ali ibn Abi Talib]] — patience and sacrificial endurance
