**Barakah** (بركة) means divine blessing, increase, or expansion — the quality of getting more out of something than its apparent measure would allow.

## Barakah in time — the core teaching

[[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] draws a direct parallel between [[sadaqah]] and time:
- Charity does not decrease wealth (established Prophetic principle) → **Time given for Allah does not decrease your time**
- Those who prioritise prayer, Quran, and worship find their remaining hours expand in productivity and quality

The Salaf narrations of reading the entire Quran in a single day are not exaggerations — they reflect this expansion of barakah in time for those who gave their hours to Allah first.

## Loss of barakah (shrinkage of time)
The Prophet ﷺ warned that near the Hour, time would feel like it shrinks:
> *"A year will feel like a month, a month like a week, a week like a day, a day like an hour."*

Scholars differ on whether this is:
1. **Literal** — Allah physically compresses time (supported by the [[Dajjal]] hadith where his first day is a year)
2. **Metaphorical** — the loss of barakah makes time feel shorter; we are busy but accomplish less

The paradox of modernity: more productivity tools → less accomplished. This is the loss of barakah, not a shortage of hours.

## Practical implications
- Set time-boxed religious goals just as you set worldly deadlines
- Know the current [[Hijri calendar]] month — ignorance of your calendar is a symptom of disconnection
- Establish [[Fajr]] prayer first; all else follows
