The *Alawi sadat* (or *Ba-Alawi*) are the descendants of [[Ali ibn Abi Talib]] through [[Imam al-Husayn]] who settled in [[Hadramaut]] (southern Arabia) and became one of the most influential scholarly and spiritual lineages in the Islamic world. The founding ancestor of this Hadrami branch was [[Ahmad ibn Isa]] (known as "al-Muhajir" — the Emigrant), a descendant of the Prophet through Imam al-Husayn, who emigrated from [[Iraq]] to Hadramaut around 318 AH / 930 CE to protect his family's religion from the innovations and political turbulence of the era. His descendants — centered in the town of [[Tarim]] — produced generation after generation of scholars, [[gnostics]], and missionaries who spread Islam throughout East Africa, South Asia, and Southeast Asia. [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] belongs to this lineage. The Ba-Alawi Sufi tradition (*tariqah*) emphasizes strict adherence to the [[Ash'ari school|Ash'ari]] creed, the [[Shafi'i]] school of jurisprudence, and an inner path of spiritual formation rooted in love of the [[Prophet Muhammad]] and the [[Quran]].
