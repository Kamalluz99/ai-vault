A Large Language Model (LLM) is a deep learning model trained on vast text corpora to predict and generate human language. LLMs underpin most modern AI assistants — including [[Claude]], [[ChatGPT]], and [[Gemini]] — and are capable of reasoning, coding, summarisation, translation, and complex task execution when paired with agentic frameworks. LLMs come in two broad categories: [[frontier models]] (proprietary, API-accessed, highest capability — e.g. Claude Sonnet, GPT-4o) and [[open source models]] (locally runnable, privacy-preserving — e.g. Llama, Mistral, Qwen). Model capability scales with parameter count and training compute, though efficiency improvements (quantisation, MoE) increasingly close the gap for specific tasks.
