A *mujaddid* (plural: *mujaddidun*) is a "renewer of religion" — a figure whom God raises in each Islamic century to revive and clarify the religion for the Muslim community of that era. The concept is grounded in the hadith attributed to [[Prophet Muhammad]]: "God will raise for this community at the head of every hundred years one who will renew for it its religion." Throughout Islamic history, scholars have identified specific figures as the Mujaddid of their century — [[Imam al-Ghazali]] is widely regarded as the Mujaddid of the sixth century, and [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] as the Mujaddid of the twelfth. The role is not formal or conferred by any institution; it is recognized retrospectively by the community on the basis of the figure's scholarship, piety, influence, and the reviving effect of his work on religious life.
