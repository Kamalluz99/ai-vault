**Ittiba' al-Nabi** (اتباع النبي) — following the Prophet ﷺ — is the Quranic condition placed on attaining the love of Allah:

> *"Say: If you love Allah, then follow me, and Allah will love you."* (Surah Al-Imran 3:31)

## Why following, not just loving

Allah did not say *"If you love Allah, then love the Prophet"* — He said *follow*. The distinction is significant:
- **Loving** the Prophet is relatively easy — read about him, feel moved, attend lectures
- **Following** him requires changing your creed, your character, and your daily actions — **deen and khuluq**

## The three layers of ittiba'
1. **Aqidah** (creed) — following his belief system is the foundation
2. **Akhlaq** (character) — following his manners, interactions, and emotional intelligence
3. **Daily life** (day-to-day sunnah) — structuring your waking hours, diet, worship, and relationships as he did ﷺ

The third layer is what the lecture *A Day in the Life of Our Beloved ﷺ* is entirely about — see [[09-15-25 Mon Dr Omar Suleiman]].

## The miracle of love without sight
The Prophet ﷺ said (seven times): *"Glad tidings to those who believe in me without having seen me."* We love him across 1,500 years, without a photograph, without having met him — yet the ummah weeps at his memory. This is itself proof of his prophethood.
