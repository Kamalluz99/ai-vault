The Isra wal-Miraj (الإسراء والمعراج) is the miraculous night journey of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, referenced in [[Surah al-Isra]] (17:1). It comprised two parts: the Isra (night journey) from [[Al-Masjid al-Haram]] in Mecca to [[Masjid Al-Aqsa]] in Jerusalem, and the Mi'raj (ascension) from Al-Aqsa through the heavens to the Divine Presence. In Jerusalem, the Prophet ﷺ led all the prophets in prayer — affirming his status as the seal and imam of all prophethood. During the ascension, the five daily prayers were prescribed. The journey united all three of the sacred masjids in a single night, which is why visiting any of the three is considered a spiritual echo of the Prophet's ﷺ journey between them.
