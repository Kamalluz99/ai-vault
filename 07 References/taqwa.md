*Taqwa* (تقوى) — God-consciousness, piety, mindful caution of God — is one of the most frequently invoked concepts in the [[Quran]] and the Islamic spiritual tradition. The root *w-q-y* means "to protect" or "to guard," and *taqwa* denotes the state of guarding oneself from God's displeasure by being continually mindful of His presence, commands, and prohibitions. It encompasses both the fear of God's chastisement and the hope of His mercy, expressed in scrupulous obedience to His law and abstention from what He has forbidden.

The Quran elevates *taqwa* as the defining criterion of human worth: "Indeed the most honourable of you before God is the most mindful of Him (*atqakum*)" (49:13). It is described as the best provision for the journey to God: "Take provision; the best provision is *taqwa*" (2:197). In the Sufi tradition, *taqwa* is foundational — the precondition for all other stations and virtues. [[Sahl ibn Abd Allah al-Tustari]] is quoted in [[The Book of Assistance]]: "There is no helper but God, no guide but the Messenger of God, no provision but *taqwa*, and no work but to have [[sabr]] in these things." [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] describes the path to [[ma'rifa]] (gnosis) as requiring "adherence to *taqwa* outwardly and inwardly" as its first step. See also: [[muraqaba]], [[wara']], [[yaqin]].
