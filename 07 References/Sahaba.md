**Sahaba** (صحابة, sing. Sahabi) — the Companions of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. A Sahabi is anyone who met the Prophet ﷺ, believed in him, and died as a Muslim. Scholars estimate there were over 100,000 Sahaba.

The Sahaba are the gold standard of Muslim character. The Quran praises them (Surah Al-Fath 48:29) and the Prophet ﷺ said: *"My companions are like stars — whichever one you follow, you will be guided."*

Key distinction: they were **human**, not superhuman. [[Dr. Omar Suleiman]] emphasises this in [[09-15-25 Mon Dr Omar Suleiman]] — *"They're super motivated, they're super righteous, but they're not superhuman."* Their achievement is accessible in principle, even if not in degree.

The Quran deliberately omits Sahaba names (with the exception of [[Zayd ibn Haritha RA]]) so that the qualities described feel universally applicable rather than person-specific.
