*Mushahada* (مشاهدة) — witnessing, direct contemplation — is the Sufi term for the state of directly beholding spiritual realities with the eye of the heart, in contrast to merely believing or inferring them. The word derives from the Arabic root *sh-h-d* (to witness, to testify). Together with [[kashf]] (unveiling), *mushahada* constitutes the highest degree of [[yaqin]] (certainty) — *haqq al-yaqin* — in which the unseen (**ghayb**) is not merely known but directly perceived, the station of the Prophets and the [[siddiqun]].

[[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] refers to *kashf* and *mushahada* as the experiential mode by which the great saints "attained to the secrets of God's Mulk and Malakut" in [[The Book of Assistance]] (Chapter 1). Unlike the lower degrees of certainty which rely on argument and inference, *mushahada* is a direct inner seeing that is self-evidencing — the heart that witnesses needs no further proof. It is the fruit of complete purification of the ego and sustained devotional practice, granted ultimately by God as a gift rather than earned as a merit. See also: [[kashf]], [[fana]], [[yaqin]], [[ma'rifa]].
