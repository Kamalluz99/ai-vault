*Laylat al-Qadr* (ليلة القدر) — the Night of Destiny, the Night of Power — is the most sacred night of the Islamic year, occurring in the final ten nights of [[Ramadan]]. The [[Quran]] devotes an entire chapter to it (Surah al-Qadr, 97), declaring: "The Night of Destiny is better than a thousand months. The angels and the Spirit descend therein, by the permission of their Lord, with all decrees. It is peace until the rising of the dawn." The Quran also describes it as "the blessed night in which all affairs are wisely decided" (44:4).

The Prophet did not specify its precise date, a wisdom attributed to ensuring that believers devote themselves fully to God throughout all the final ten nights rather than only on one. Most scholars hold it most likely falls in the odd-numbered nights of the last ten — the 21st, 23rd, 25th, 27th, or 29th of Ramadan — with the 27th being the most commonly cited. The Prophet intensified his worship during this period and encouraged his community to do the same, including [[i'tikaf]] (spiritual retreat in the mosque). [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] discusses it in Chapter 19 of [[The Book of Assistance]], noting that some gnostics have witnessed it experientially — perceiving the blazing lights, the open doors of heaven, and the angels ascending and descending.
