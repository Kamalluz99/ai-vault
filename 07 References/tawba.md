*Tawba* (توبة) — repentance — is the turning of the servant back to God from sin. The Arabic root *t-w-b* means "to return," and the term applies both to the servant's turning toward God and to God's turning (of mercy and acceptance) toward the servant. Classical scholars define complete *tawba* as requiring three conditions: (1) *iqla'* — immediately ceasing the sin; (2) *nadam* — genuine remorse for having committed it; and (3) *'azm* — firm resolve not to return to it. If the sin involved another person's rights (property, honour, life), a fourth condition applies: rectifying the wrong or seeking the wronged party's forgiveness.

[[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] teaches in [[The Book of Assistance]] that *tawba* is obligatory immediately upon every sin — there is no license for delay. God loves those who repent — "Indeed God loves those who are perpetually repentant" (Quran 2:222) — and the door of tawba remains open until the final breaths and the rising of the sun from the West. The *Hadith Qudsi* is cited: "O My servants! You do wrong night and day, and I forgive all sins — so seek My forgiveness and I shall forgive you." *Tawba* is paired with [[khawf]] (fear) and [[raja']] (hope) as the three foundational orientations of the heart in Chapter 27.
