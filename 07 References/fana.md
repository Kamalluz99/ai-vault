*Fana* (فناء) — annihilation, extinction — is the Sufi term for the dissolution of the ego's self-centered perspective in the overwhelming awareness of God. The companion concept *baqa'* (subsistence) refers to the life that remains after *fana* — the servant subsists, but now his existence is oriented entirely by God rather than by self. [[Al-Junayd]] is credited with giving these concepts their classical formulation, distinguishing the orthodox Sufi meaning (transformation of the ego's passions and self-interest, not the literal extinction of the person) from heterodox interpretations that suggest the creature merges into or becomes identical with the Creator — which would be [[hulul]] (indwelling) or [[ittihad]] (union), both rejected as theologically incorrect.

In practice, *fana* describes the experiential state in which a servant is so absorbed in awareness of God — through sustained [[dhikr]], [[muraqaba]], and [[tafakkur]] — that consciousness of created things recedes and God alone occupies the heart. [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] alludes to it in [[The Book of Assistance]] (Chapters 3 and 9) as the ultimate fruit of [[muraqaba]]: "totally immersed in God, annihilated in Him, and thus rendered unaware of all else, absent from creation through his contemplation of the True King, having arrived at a secure seat in the presence of an Able Sovereign." See also: [[mahabba]], [[muraqaba]], [[kashf]].
