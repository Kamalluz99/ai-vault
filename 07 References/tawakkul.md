*Tawakkul* (توكل) — reliance on God, trust in God — is the complete dependence of the servant on God for all affairs, combined with the understanding that taking lawful means is itself part of *tawakkul*, not a contradiction of it. The classic illustration is the hadith: "Tie your camel, then put your trust in God." [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] describes it in Chapter 31 of [[The Book of Assistance]] and identifies three levels: the lowest resembles a child's trust in its mother (the servant acts but knows the outcome is fully in God's hands); the middle resembles a patient trusting a wise physician (doing what is prescribed, then releasing anxiety); the highest resembles a corpse in the hands of the washer — fully receptive and free of self-direction.

*Tawakkul* is the fruit of [[yaqin]] (certainty): only the servant who is genuinely certain of God's sufficiency, goodness, and control can genuinely rely on Him without anxiety. Its practical signs include: not panicking over provision, not hoarding beyond need, being at peace with what God sends, and focusing entirely on the present obligation rather than future uncertainties. The Quranic promise: "And whoever puts his trust in God — He shall suffice him" (65:3). See also: [[yaqin]], [[rida]], [[sabr]].
