A *Hadith Qudsi* (حديث قدسي — "Sacred Hadith" or "Divine Hadith") is a category of hadith in which the Prophet conveys the direct speech of God, but which is not part of the [[Quran]]. Unlike the Quran — whose exact wording and arrangement are considered the miraculous, verbatim speech of God — a *Hadith Qudsi* conveys the *meaning* of divine speech through the Prophet's own words. The chain of transmission (*isnad*) leads back to the Prophet, who is quoting God; the Prophet's phrasing is not itself divinely protected as the Quran's wording is. Classical examples include: "I am as My servant thinks Me to be, and I am with him when he remembers Me" (cited in [[The Book of Assistance]] on [[dhikr]]); "Fasting is Mine and I shall reward him for it" (cited on fasting); and "My servant draws nearer to Me with nothing more pleasing to Me than what I have made obligatory upon him…" (cited on religious obligations and sainthood). The term distinguishes these divine-speech narrations from both the Quran and ordinary hadiths (prophetic reports not framed as divine speech).
