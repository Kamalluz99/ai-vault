*I Am a Strange Loop* is a 2007 book by [[Douglas Hofstadter]], a follow-up to his Pulitzer Prize-winning *Gödel, Escher, Bach*. It argues that consciousness and the sense of "I" arise from self-referential feedback loops in the brain — patterns that perceive and represent themselves. Hofstadter uses the mathematics of [[Gödel's incompleteness theorems]] as an analogy: just as formal systems can contain statements about themselves, brains contain symbols that refer to the very system generating them. Dense and philosophical, it rewards re-reading.
