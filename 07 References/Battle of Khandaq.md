The Battle of Khandaq (غزوة الخندق — Battle of the Trench, also called Al-Ahzab/The Confederates) took place in 5 AH (627 CE) when a coalition of Meccan and tribal forces laid siege to [[Medina]]. Acting on the suggestion of [[Salman al-Farisi (ra)]], the Muslims dug a defensive trench around the vulnerable northern approaches to the city — a tactic unknown to the Arabs at the time. During the digging, the Prophet ﷺ struck a stone and three lights emerged: the first revealed the palaces of Persia, the second the palaces of the Byzantines and [[Al-Sham]], and the third the palaces of Ethiopia and Yemen — a prophetic vision of Islam spreading across the known world. The siege was ultimately broken after weeks, partly due to a rift sown between the confederate factions.
