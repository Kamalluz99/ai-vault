The Qibla (القبلة) is the direction Muslims face during prayer ([[Salah]]). The current Qibla is toward [[Al-Masjid al-Haram]] in [[Mecca]], specifically the [[Ka'bah]]. For the first 13–17 years of Islam (approximately 610–624 CE), the Qibla was directed toward [[Bayt al-Maqdis]] (Jerusalem). The change was made by divine command, revealed in [[Surah al-Baqarah]] (2:144), while the Prophet ﷺ was in Medina. The original orientation toward Jerusalem was deliberate — to embed [[Al-Quds]] permanently in the hearts of Muslims as a sacred direction of prayer, before the final Qibla was established toward Mecca.
