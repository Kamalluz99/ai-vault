*Mahabba* (محبة) — love of God — is regarded in the Sufi tradition as the crown and goal of all spiritual stations, the summit toward which [[yaqin]], [[muraqaba]], [[tawba]], [[sabr]], [[shukr]], [[zuhd]], and [[tawakkul]] all ascend. The Quran establishes that God's love is conditional on following the Prophet: "Say: If you love God then follow me, and God will love you" (3:31) — making emulation of the [[Prophet Muhammad]] the indispensable expression of divine love.

[[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] treats it as the final chapter of [[The Book of Assistance]] (Chapter 32), alongside [[rida]]. The signs of true *mahabba*: finding worship sweet rather than burdensome; feeling longing as the prayer time approaches; preferring solitude with God over the company of creation; being indifferent to worldly praise or blame; grieving at every moment of distance from God. *Mahabba* and [[dhikr]] are inseparable — love drives remembrance and remembrance deepens love. The station above *mahabba* — or sometimes identified as its fullness — is [[rida]] (contentment with God's decree), where the servant's will has aligned completely with God's will.
