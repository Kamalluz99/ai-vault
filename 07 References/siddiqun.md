*Siddiqun* (صدّيقون, singular: *siddiq*) — the Truthful, the Sincere, the Utterly Faithful — is a Quranic category for the highest rank of human spiritual excellence, below the Prophets. The Quran places them alongside the Prophets, the martyrs, and the righteous: "Whoever obeys God and the Messenger, those are with the ones whom God has blessed: the Prophets, the *siddiqun*, the martyrs, and the righteous" (4:69). The term is rooted in *sidq* (truth/sincerity) — a *siddiq* is one whose inner reality perfectly matches his outward expression, whose belief is fully verified by his state, and whose certainty ([[yaqin]]) has reached the degree of *'ayn al-yaqin* (direct witnessing) or beyond.

In Sufi terminology, *siddiqun* are the Perfect Saints — those whose certainty has reached the level where the unseen becomes as the seen, which is the state of [[kashf]] (unveiling) and [[mushahada]] (contemplation). [[Imam Abdallah al-Haddad]] uses the term throughout [[The Book of Assistance]] to denote those who have fully traversed the spiritual path: they are the "Ones Brought Nigh" (*muqarrabun*), the heirs of the Prophets, and the highest category of *wali* (saint) short of prophethood. The first Caliph [[Abu Bakr al-Siddiq]] bears *al-Siddiq* as his permanent epithet — given by the Prophet himself — as the paradigm of this quality among the Companions.
