Spaced repetition is a learning technique that schedules reviews of information at expanding intervals — reviewing material just before you would forget it. The spacing effect, first described by Hermann Ebbinghaus, shows that information reviewed at increasing gaps is retained far more efficiently than massed practice (cramming). Modern implementations like [[Anki]] use the SM-2 algorithm or variants to calculate optimal review intervals per card based on self-reported recall difficulty. Widely used in medical education, language acquisition, and any domain requiring retention of large, precise fact sets.
